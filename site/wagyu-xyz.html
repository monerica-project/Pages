    Wagyu fills a critical gap cause it demystifies access to Monero for the average trader. YES ofc it operates via a bridge to a public ledger (Hyperliquid here) which introduces a different trust assumption than a native atomic swap BUT complaining that a bridge has bridgelike risks (centralization for example) is like complaining that a hammer isn't a screwdriver

It brings volume, it brings fresh liquidity, and it makes Monero usable for people who aren't CLI wizards. Im rating this 5/5 not cause its "perfect" privacy, but its the most effective tool in its specific category right now.
